Graph Descriptions

1. Long-Term Trends in Female Homicide Counts

File: long_term_female_homicide_counts.html

What the figure shows:
This visualisation presents the absolute number of female homicide victims per year in Ireland, South Africa, and the United States from 2015 onwards. Each line represents a country and shows annual victim counts over time.

Major findings:

  • South Africa reports the highest number of victims, often above 2,500 per year.
  • The United States shows large numbers as well, between 3,300 and 5,000 yearly.
  • Ireland’s counts remain extremely low (typically 2–15 victims per year).
  • South Africa and the U.S. show noticeable increases in recent years.

Data used: ireland_female_homicide_counts.csv, south_africa_female_homicide_counts.csv, united_states_female_homicide_counts.csv

2. Long-Term Trends in Female Homicide Rates

File: long_term_female_homicide_rates.html

What the figure shows:
This chart shows annual femicide rates per 100,000 women, enabling a population-adjusted comparison between countries.

Major findings:

  • South Africa consistently has the highest rates (often above 9 per 100,000).
  • The United States shows medium rates (around 2–3 per 100,000).
  • Ireland has very low rates (below 1 per 100,000).
  • South Africa’s rates rise sharply after 2021.

Data used: ireland_female_homicide_rates.csv, south_africa_female_homicide_rates.csv, united_states_female_homicide_rates.csv

3. Media Mentions of “Femicide” and “Gender-Based Violence”

File: media_mentions_femicide_gbv_bar_chart.html

What the figure shows:
This chart aggregates yearly media mentions of “femicide” and “gender-based violence” across Ireland, South Africa, and the United States.

Major findings:

  • The United States shows the highest media attention, especially for gender-based violence.
  • South Africa also shows high media coverage but below U.S. levels.
  • Ireland shows low counts but rises after 2020.
  • “Gender-based violence” consistently exceeds “femicide” in all countries.

Data used: Irland_femicide.csv.csv, Irland_gender_based_violence.csv.csv, Sydafrika_femicide.csv.csv, Sydafrika_gender_based_violence.csv.csv, USA_femicide.csv.csv, USA_gender_based_violence.csv.csv

4. Multi-Country Femicide and SDG 5 Overlay

File: multi_country_femicide_sdg5_overlay_US_included.html

What the figure shows:
A dual-axis visualisation plotting SDG 5 gender equality scores (left axis) against femicide rates (right axis) for three countries.

Major findings:

  • Ireland and the U.S. improve steadily on SDG 5 but do not show a corresponding decline in femicide.
  • South Africa has fluctuating SDG 5 scores and high femicide levels.
  • Institutional gender equality does not directly correlate with lower femicide.

Data used: sdg_data.csv + homicide rate files for all three countries

5. SDG Goal 5 Progress Over Time

File: sdg_goal5_progress_over_time.html

What the figure shows:
A long-term visualisation of gender equality (SDG 5) scores from 2000 to 2022.

Major findings:

  • Ireland and the U.S. show steady improvement.
  • South Africa shows more variability, including declines around 2018–2020.
  • By 2022, all countries converge near similar SDG 5 levels.

Data used: sdg_data.csv

6. Relationship Between SDG 5 and Femicide Rates (Regression)

File: sdg5_vs_female_homicide_rate_regression.html

What the figure shows:
A scatter plot with an OLS regression exploring the relationship between institutional gender equality (SDG 5) and femicide rates.

Major findings:

  • Ireland: high SDG 5, low femicide.
  • South Africa: high SDG 5, but very high femicide.
  • United States: in between but variable.
  • Regression line slopes upward → higher SDG 5 does not predict lower femicide.
  • Weak R² (~0.28).

Data used: sdg_data.csv + homicide rate files for all countries
